About

The annual medical conference is one of the most important and valuable medical meeting in our country which offer our colleagues a comprehensive array of educational and networking opportunities designed specifically for the internal medicine professionals.

As our previous conferences, the 8th Conference on Internal Medicine aims to re-energize and focus our practice with the latest research, best practices and newest innovations in the field that can immediately be applied to improving patient care. Additionally, we are looking forward to learning from the “best of the best,” including renowned leaders in the field of internal medicine, and collaborating with a vibrant community of internal medicine professionals.

The conference will focus on the following scientific topics:

Academic and clinical research

This track is for internists who are interested in clinical audits and researches with special priority to medical fields.

Clinical updates and guidelines

The clinical updates track will cover evidence-based updates and guidelines from recent literatures published in medicine subspecialty fields and specific topic areas that every internist needs to know in order to provide optimal management for hospitalized patients.

Diagnostic reasoning

Accurate and timely diagnosis are the cornerstones of high-quality patient care. The conference will give the priority to topics in: diagnostic errors, solutions of diagnostic errors, and teaching clinical reasoning.

Case reports

Sessions of this topic will give a great opportunity to our colleagues to present rare clinical cases which were evaluated and managed in our local hospitals.

Practice management and quality

Given the importance of the quality and the safety of patient in the delivery of healthcare. This track is offered to address the imperatives around development and implementation of improvement efforts in our hospitals.

High value care

Unnecessary care is not only wasteful and costly, but it can be harmful to patients as well! Our High Value Care (HVC) track will guide attendees on how to avoid diagnostic and therapeutic overuse and how to move toward the sensible care for every hospitalized patient.

Submission Form

Before submitting your paper, Please follow these guidelines:

  • Abstracts should be between 300 and 500 words, and written in English with clear and informative content.
  • All Abstracts of clinical research and audits must be structured in subheadings as follow:
    A. Background, B. Objectives, C. Methods, D. Results and Interpretation, E. Conclusion, F. Recommendations (whenever appropriate).
    while case report subheadings are:
    A. Background, B. Case presentation, C. Clinical workup and intervention, D. Discussion, E. Conclusion.
  • Abstracts should also include the full names and affiliations of all abstract authors.
  • Abstracts must be typed in Times New Romans font, with abstract title size 14 point, and abstract main body 12 point.
  • Abstracts must be formatted as a Microsoft Word document.
  • ABSTRACTS WHICH DO NOT ADHERE TO THE THESE GIUDELINES ARE AT HIGH RISK OF REJECTION.
